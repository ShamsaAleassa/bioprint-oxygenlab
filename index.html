Physical Model

Michaelis-Menten Diffusion-Consumption

Version 2 replaces first-order linear consumption with the physiologically correct Michaelis-Menten kinetics, real O₂ concentration units, and tissue-specific transport parameters drawn from peer-reviewed literature.

∂C/∂t = D∇²C − Q_max · C / (K_m + C)
C = oxygen concentration (µM)  ·  D = diffusion coefficient (m²/s)  ·  Q_max = maximum volumetric consumption rate (µM/s)  ·  K_m = half-saturation constant (µM, typically 1–5 µM for mammalian cells)

When C ≫ K_m the consumption approaches Q_max (zero-order, constant drain). When C ≪ K_m it reduces to first-order (linear), with effective rate constant k = Q_max/K_m. The characteristic penetration length is λ = √(D · K_m / Q_max) in the linear regime.
C_diff = C + α·∇²_h C  ·  C_new = C_diff / (1 + Δt · Q_max / (K_m + C_diff))
Operator splitting: explicit diffusion step followed by an implicit (backward-Euler) consumption step. The implicit treatment makes the consumption term unconditionally stable regardless of Q_max magnitude — critical for high-metabolism tissues such as cardiac muscle where explicit methods would require Δt < 10⁻⁴ s. Diffusion number α = D·Δt/Δx² ≤ 0.25 ensures diffusion stability (Δt = 0.05 s, Δx = 25 µm).
C_vessel = C_source · P_v / (P_v + D/Δx)
Robin boundary condition (vessel wall permeability): the oxygen concentration at a vessel surface is not simply the blood/medium concentration — it is attenuated by the vessel wall resistance. P_v is the wall permeability coefficient (m/s). Open bioprinted channels: P_v → ∞ → C_vessel = C_source. Endothelialised capillaries: P_v ≈ 10⁻⁴ m/s, D/Δx ≈ 4×10⁻⁵ m/s → C_vessel ≈ 0.71 × C_source. PDMS walls: C_vessel ≈ 0.33 × C_source.

Why Michaelis-Menten Matters

First-order consumption (linear k·C) overestimates depletion near vessels (where C ≫ K_m) and underestimates it in hypoxic zones. Michaelis-Menten correctly captures saturation: cells consume at full rate Q_max even at moderate O₂, creating sharper transition zones and smaller necrotic cores than linear models predict.

O₂ Unit Conversion

  • C (µM) ÷ 1.27 = PO₂ (mmHg) at 37 °C
  • Arterial blood: ~200 µM (~100 mmHg)
  • Venous return: ~100 µM (~40 mmHg)
  • Hypoxia onset: 5–20 µM (tissue-type dependent)
  • Necrosis: <1–5 µM
  • 21% O₂ bioreactor: ~215 µM

3-D Depth Model

For a slab of thickness L with surface O₂ = C_surf, the 1-D analytical steady-state gives a central minimum: C_center = C_surf / cosh(L / 2λ). The viable depth from each surface is: z_hyp = λ · arccosh(C_surf / C_hypoxia). The 3-D viable volume is then computed by integrating this correction over all (x,y) tissue cells.

Tissue-Specific Thresholds

  • Cartilage: C_hyp = 5 µM, C_nec = 0.5 µM (adapted to low O₂)
  • Liver: C_hyp = 10 µM, C_nec = 2 µM
  • Cardiac: C_hyp = 15 µM, C_nec = 3 µM
  • MSC: C_hyp = 5 µM, C_nec = 0.5 µM
  • Custom: user-adjustable via presets panel

Remaining Limitations vs. Full Clinical Validation

  • 2-D planar model — 3-D correction is analytical (1-D slab) not full volumetric FEM
  • Homogeneous cell density — no spatial variation in seeding or ECM microstructure
  • Steady-state vessel O₂ — no pulsatile flow, vessel wall pressure, or axial O₂ depletion along vessel length
  • No CO₂ / lactate / pH transport — waste accumulation can independently trigger apoptosis
  • No angiogenesis, cell migration, scaffold degradation, or mechanobiological coupling
  • D assumed isotropic and homogeneous — fibre-reinforced or layered scaffolds have anisotropic D
  • All verdicts are model-based educational thresholds — regulatory submission requires validated 3-D FEM + in vitro/in vivo correlation
